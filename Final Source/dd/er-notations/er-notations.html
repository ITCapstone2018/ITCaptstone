Introduction

Components and Symbols

Every E-R diagram consists of several components: entities, attributes, identifiers, and relationships. It is possible to diagram these components using one of many methods of E-R Diagramming. Even though there is not one universal, acceptable method to E-R Diagramming, many of the symbols used remain consistent. First of all, classes are always denoted by rectangles. Lines are then drawn between the classes to depict a link between two classes. Often, a diamond or triangle will be drawn on this line that tells how the two classes are related. Finally, E-R Diagrams use symbols to show cardinality. These symbols hardly ever remain consistent, however, and can be numbers, hash marks, diamonds, or, most commonly, crow's feet.





Notation Sets Defined - Crow's Feet Minimum to Maximum

There are many ways to depict an E-R Diagram, and, as such, this page of the introduction will show and describe the Crow's Feet and Minimum to Maximum methods of showing the same 1:N relationship.



Crow's Feet

- Uses crow's feet to show cardinality. 
- Shows mandatory (|) or optional (O) values.



Minimum to Maximum

- Shows numerical minimum and maximum on both sides of the relationship.



Notation Sets Defined - IDEF1X & Class

There are many ways to depict an E-R Diagram, and, as such, this page of the introduction will show and describe the IDEF1X and Class methods of showing the same 1:N relationship.



IDEF1X

Class

- Used by the US Government. 
- Uses open or closed circles to show cardinality.

- Cardinality is shown with Minimum to Maximum values, separated        by an ellipsis. 
- A triangle is used to denote the relationship.
