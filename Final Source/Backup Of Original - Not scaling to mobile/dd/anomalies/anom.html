Introduction

Anomalies

Database anomalies are the problems that occur in an un-normalized database table due to data redundancy. There are three different types of database anomalies - Insert, Update, and Delete. Insert anomalies occur when a new record is inserted into a table. This type of anomaly can be avoided by ensuring that any records added to a table are both correct and complete. Update anomalies occur when a record is modified. In other words, this anomaly will occur if a specific instance is altered in the table, but not in its every occurrence. Therefore, this type of anomaly can be avoided by ensuring that any update of a specific instance is completely updated in all of its accuracies. Delete anomalies occur when a record is deleted from a table. Specifically, this anomaly occurs when the deletion of one record affects the integrity of other records.
